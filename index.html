UVC Disinfectant System

Since its invention in early 1900, UV lamps have become a staple method of Air, Water & Surface sterilization - one used in hospitals, airplanes, offices and factories every day. UVC rays of the lamps we use have wavelength ranging from 232 nano meters to 254 nano meters and are extremely lethal for Viruses but are not meant for any kind of Exposure to Humans or Animals. UVC Ray Kills 99.99% of Germs, Bacteria & Viruses. These lamps were effectively & widely used during the SARS Coronavirus Pandemic. The radiation wraps the structure of their genetic material and prevents the viral parcticles from replicating. That means they could be used in closed and crowded spaces where contamination risks run high, with potentially huge promise for use during the current pandemic. Also, a FAR-UVC lamp which has wavelength shorter than 222 nano meters is deemed safe for Human Exposure and is produced by a company in the United States of America but currently unavailable in India for Commercial Use.
